# Phase 17.1 Plan 02: Kitchen Consumer/Aggregate Hardening Summary

**Kitchen items now persist advancedBy/advancedAt on every valid advance, the kitchen ledger insert runs in an isolated REQUIRES_NEW writer bean, and a same-order OrderConfirmed re-emitted under a new eventId is absorbed as a logged no-op instead of hitting the DLT.**

## Accomplishments
- KitchenTicketItemEntity gained nullable `advanced_by` (UUID) and `advanced_at` (Instant) columns; KitchenTicketAdvanceService.advance() sets both immediately after the status transition and before save, giving every item an accountability trail of who advanced it and when (K-WR-02)
- New KitchenLedgerWriter `@Component` with `@Transactional(propagation = Propagation.REQUIRES_NEW)` isolates the kitchen idempotency-ledger insert from KitchenTicketCreationService's business transaction — a concurrent-duplicate violation no longer marks ticket creation rollback-only (I-WR-01, kitchen side)
- KitchenTicketCreationService now checks `existsByOrderId` before building a ticket, absorbing a same-order OrderConfirmed re-emitted under a fresh eventId as a logged no-op instead of throwing to the DLT on the unique orderId constraint (K-IN-01); the dead `findByOrderId` repository method was removed (K-IN-02)

## Decisions Made
- Audit trail as two nullable item columns rather than a separate audit table (research recommendation, "Claude's Discretion" per plan) — matches existing single-row-per-item design and avoids new schema surface for a simple accountability requirement.
- KitchenLedgerWriter is a byte-for-byte structural mirror of InventoryLedgerWriter (REQUIRES_NEW `tryInsert(eventId, consumerName)` returning boolean, DataIntegrityViolationException → debug log + false) to keep the I-WR-01 pattern consistent across contexts.
